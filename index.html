⚖️ Your Legal Rights After Retrenchment
📋 What Your Employer MUST Pay
Accrued salary
Up to your last day worked — must be paid in full
Accrued annual leave
Unused leave days must be paid out
Notice pay
If you were not given proper notice, you get paid-in-lieu (1 month per standard contract)
Termination benefits (severance)
Default JTAS rates:
• 0–2 years: 10 days' pay per year
• 2–5 years: 15 days' pay per year
• 5+ years: 20 days' pay per year
Union/Collective Agreements may offer better rates
⏰ Deadlines You Need to Know
⚠️ 7 days
Employer must pay ALL monies owed within 7 days of termination date
⏱️ 60 days
File your SOCSO EIS claim within 60 days of losing. After 60 days, you lose eligibility.
✅ EA Form
Request your EA Form from your employer before you leave. You need it for tax filing.
📅 Dispute
If your employer disputes the severance, you can file with the Director-General of the Labour Department. They can force arbitration within 30 days.
🏛️ SOCSO / EIS (Employment Insurance System)
If you contributed to SOCSO, you're eligible for Job Search Allowance and Temporary Employment Allowance through the EIS scheme (also known as SIP Prihatin).

💰 EPF (KWSP) & Tax
EPF Akaun 2 Withdrawal: If you're retrenched, you can apply for an early withdrawal from Akaun 2 (under the Retrenched Worker Scheme). This is separate from the RM500 one-time withdrawal.
Termination benefits are NOT taxable: In Malaysia, termination/retirement compensation is exempt from income tax up to RM10,000 per year of service after 1999. For most people, severance is completely tax-free.
🔍 Common Employer Violations (Watch Out)
  • If your employer says "we don't have a severance policy" — default JTAS rates still apply by law
  • If they try to classify you as a "contractor" to avoid SOCSO — check your employment status
  • If they deduct money from your severance — they can only deduct for specific things (EPF, tax, court orders)
  • If they don't pay within 7 days — file a complaint with the Labour Department immediately
  • "Mutual separation" means you sign an agreement — don't sign without understanding the terms

Annual tax form showing all earnings. Required for tax filing and SOCSO claim.

Shows your current balance across Akaun Persaraan, Akaun Sejahtera, and Akaun Fleksibel.
Termination / Retrenchment Letter
Official letter from employer stating reason and date of termination. Keep this for proof.
Last 3 months payslips
Needed for SOCSO EIS claim to verify your average monthly salary.

For SOCSO claim identification
Bank account details
For SOCSO payments to be deposited into. Must be your own account.
